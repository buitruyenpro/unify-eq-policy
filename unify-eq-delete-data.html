Account & Data Deletion

This page applies to UNIFY Equalizer Bass Booster (also referred to as UNIFY EQ). Instructions for deleting optional account information, clearing local equalizer and player data, and managing purchases.

01Choose the action you need

ActionResult
Delete the optional UNIFY accountRemoves account, profile and community information stored for UNIFY EQ, subject to limited legal or security retention.

03What is deleted from an optional account

  • Profile information such as name, email, avatar, username, biography, country and optional creator fields.
  • Published presets, Music Editor Pro segment configurations, generated covers and associated creator content.
  • Your comments, likes, favorites, follows, reposts, reports and Community notifications where deletion is technically and legally permitted.
  • Account-linked preferences and marketing-choice records where applicable.
  • Other personal information not required for legal, fraud-prevention, moderation or billing purposes.

Content already reshared by another user may be removed, de-linked or anonymized depending on the feature and applicable requirements.

04What may be retained

  • Anonymized or aggregated statistics that no longer identify you.
  • Purchase or transaction records required for accounting, tax, fraud prevention or legal compliance.
  • Short-lived security and diagnostic records needed to protect the service and investigate faults.
  • A minimal suppression record where needed to honor a marketing opt-out.

05Remove local app data

Deleting an online account does not remove original audio files stored on your phone or SD card. It also may not automatically remove presets or configurations already downloaded to the device. To remove locally generated UNIFY EQ data:

  1. Delete presets, downloaded Community items, profiles, playlists or editor projects inside UNIFY EQ where available.
  2. Open Android Settings → Apps → UNIFY EQ → Storage.
  3. Choose Clear storage/data, or uninstall the app.

This can remove local databases, settings, downloaded presets, preferences, artwork references, equalizer profiles and locally exported or imported configuration data stored in the app area. Original music files remain untouched unless you separately delete them.

06Google Play subscriptions and purchases

Deleting an account or uninstalling UNIFY EQ does not cancel a Google Play subscription.

Cancel an active subscription from Google Play → Payments & subscriptions → Subscriptions. Purchase records and eligible entitlements are controlled by the Google Play account used for the purchase.
