Privacy Policy

This policy explains what UNIFY Music accesses, why it is needed, where data is processed and the controls available to you.

Last updated · July 16, 2026 · Version 2.0

01Introduction

UNIFY Hi-Res Music Player Pro is an Android music player operated by an independent developer in Vietnam. It plays music stored on your device and can connect to personal Google Drive, Dropbox, WebDAV and SMB/NAS sources.

UNIFY does not provide a public streaming catalog or music downloads. This policy applies to the Android app and this website.

Core principle: local music remains on your device. Connected-source music is transferred directly from the selected provider or private server to your Android device for library scanning, metadata, playback and local caching.

02Local media and Android permissions

Data or permissionPurpose
Audio and media filesScan, organize and play music stored on your phone or supported SD card. We may read filenames, paths, tags, duration and embedded artwork.
Notifications and foreground playbackShow playback controls and keep music playing when the app is in the background.
Audio processing accessProvide equalizer, visualizer and sound-effect features on the device. UNIFY does not use this to create voice recordings.
Local app storageStore playlists, settings, artwork, metadata, source indexes and cached audio ranges.

Original local audio files are not uploaded to a developer-operated server.

03Connected music sources

Google Drive

When you connect Google Drive, UNIFY may access the files and folders needed to browse the location you select and play supported audio. This can include file and folder names, IDs, folder structure, MIME types, sizes, modification information, audio content, tags and embedded artwork.

Google Drive data is used only to provide folder browsing, library indexing, metadata display, streaming, seeking and local caching. Music is transferred directly between Google Drive and your device; UNIFY does not upload Drive files to a developer-operated server.

Google API Limited Use: UNIFY Music's use and transfer of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

Dropbox

With your permission, UNIFY may read basic account identity, selected folder structure, file metadata and audio content required to scan and play your Dropbox music. OAuth tokens are used only for the connected Dropbox feature.

WebDAV and SMB/NAS

You provide the server address and, where required, credentials. The app connects directly from your device to your server or NAS. Credentials are stored locally using Android security mechanisms and are not included in music URLs or cache identifiers.

Local cache and source indexes

UNIFY may store metadata, artwork, source indexes and partial or complete audio cache files on your device. This improves startup, seeking and offline reuse. You can clear cached data in the app or through Android app settings.

Disconnecting access

Disconnecting a source stops new access and removes the locally stored authorization for that source. Previously indexed metadata or cached audio may remain until you clear the source library, clear cache, clear app data or uninstall UNIFY. You may also revoke Google or Dropbox access from your provider account settings.

04Optional accounts and community features

The core player can be used without creating a UNIFY community account. If you sign in for optional profile, preset-sharing or synchronization features, we may process:

  • Name, email address, profile image and authentication provider.
  • A unique account identifier, account timestamps and optional profile fields.
  • Published equalizer presets, creator cards, feedback, saves, subscriptions and related activity.
  • Your Premium entitlement status, without receiving your payment-card details.

Account and community information may be stored using Google Firebase services. Public profile or published preset information is visible to other users only when you choose to make it public.

05Diagnostics, analytics, advertising and billing

  • Diagnostics: app version, device/app state and crash information may be processed to maintain stability and security.
  • Analytics: aggregate or pseudonymous usage events may be used to understand feature performance.
  • Advertising: the free version may display ads where enabled. Ad providers may process device or advertising identifiers according to their own policies and applicable consent settings.
  • Billing: purchases are processed by Google Play. UNIFY receives purchase status or entitlement information, not full payment-card details.

UNIFY does not sell your personal music files, cloud files, server credentials or email address.

06When information is shared

Information may be processed by service providers that support app operation, such as Google Firebase, Google Play, Google APIs, Dropbox APIs, crash/analytics services and advertising services where enabled. These providers process information under their own terms and privacy commitments.

We may disclose information when required by law, to protect users or the service, or during a legitimate business transfer. We do not share personal music files with advertisers or data brokers.

07Security, storage and retention

  • OAuth tokens and private-server credentials are stored locally using available Android security features.
  • Network traffic uses provider-supported encrypted connections. For WebDAV, HTTPS is recommended; HTTP connections may be less secure.
  • Local caches remain until cleared, removed by Android storage management or deleted with the app.
  • Account data is retained while the account is active and for a reasonable period needed for security, legal, billing or deletion processing.
  • Diagnostic logs and anonymized statistics may be retained for a limited operational period.

No security method is perfect. Keep your device, cloud account and private server credentials protected.

08Your choices and controls

  • Use the core local player without creating a community account.
  • Choose which cloud folder or private server to connect.
  • Disconnect Google Drive, Dropbox, WebDAV or SMB from within UNIFY.
  • Revoke Google or Dropbox authorization from the provider's account settings.
  • Clear cached files and local app data.
  • Request access, correction or deletion of optional account data.

Account and data deletion instructions are available at unifyplayer.online/unify-music-delete-data.html.

09Children's privacy

UNIFY is not directed to children. The optional online account and community features are intended for users aged 16 or older. If you believe a younger person has provided personal information, contact us so the account can be reviewed and removed where appropriate.

10Changes and contact

We may update this policy when features, providers or legal requirements change. The current version and update date will remain available on this page.
