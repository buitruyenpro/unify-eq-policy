Account & Data Deletion

Instructions for deleting optional UNIFY account data, disconnecting music sources and clearing local app data.

Last updated · July 16, 2026

01Choose the action you need

ActionResult
Disconnect a music sourceStops future access to Google Drive, Dropbox, WebDAV or SMB. It does not delete your UNIFY community account.
Delete the UNIFY accountRemoves optional account, profile and community data stored for UNIFY.
Clear app data or uninstallRemoves local settings, databases, source indexes and caches from the Android device. It does not delete original files from your phone, cloud drive or private server.
Account deletion is irreversible. Published presets, profile content and other account data may be permanently removed or anonymized.

03Disconnect connected music sources

Google Drive

Use the UNIFY Music Sources screen to disconnect Google Drive. You can also revoke UNIFY Music from your Google Account's third-party connections. Revoking access stops future Drive requests but does not automatically delete a separate UNIFY community account.

Dropbox

Disconnect Dropbox inside UNIFY or remove the app from the connected-apps area of your Dropbox account.

WebDAV and SMB/NAS

Remove the connection inside UNIFY. This deletes the saved connection and locally stored authorization for that source. It does not delete files from your server or NAS.

Previously indexed metadata, artwork or cached audio may remain on the device until you clear the relevant source data, clear cache, clear Android app data or uninstall UNIFY.

04What is deleted from an optional UNIFY account

  • Account profile information such as name, email, avatar, username and optional profile fields.
  • Published presets, creator cards and associated profile content.
  • Feedback, saves, creator subscriptions and account notifications.
  • Account-linked preferences and marketing choice records where applicable.
  • Other personal information not required to be retained for legal, fraud-prevention or billing purposes.

05What may be retained

  • Anonymized or aggregated statistics that no longer identify you.
  • Purchase or transaction records required for accounting, tax, fraud prevention or legal compliance.
  • Short-lived security and diagnostic records needed to protect the service and investigate faults.
  • A minimal suppression record where necessary to honor a request not to receive future marketing messages.

Where retention is required, information is kept only for the applicable purpose and period.

06Local music, playlists and cached data

Deleting an online account does not delete the original music files stored on your phone, SD card, Google Drive, Dropbox, WebDAV server or SMB/NAS share.

UNIFY settings, local playlists, databases, artwork and cached audio may remain on the device. To remove them:

  1. Clear cache from UNIFY where available.
  2. Open Android Settings → Apps → UNIFY Music → Storage.
  3. Choose Clear storage/data, or uninstall the app.

07Google Play subscriptions and purchases

Deleting an account or uninstalling UNIFY does not cancel a Google Play subscription.

Cancel active Monthly or Yearly subscriptions from Google Play → Payments & subscriptions → Subscriptions. Purchase records and eligible entitlements are controlled by the Google Play account used for the purchase.
