Why Google Drive access is requested

Play music from a folder you choose

Google Drive connection is optional. When you connect it, UNIFY uses read-only access to browse a folder you select, build a music index, read metadata and embedded artwork, stream audio and keep playback cache on your Android device.

Local playback works without signing in. You can disconnect Drive in the app or revoke access from your Google Account at any time.

Everything
worth knowing.

Clear answers about connected storage, progressive cache, player themes and immersive spatial audio.

UNIFY is an Android offline music player for audio files you own or control. It scans phone storage or a supported SD card, organizes a personal library, plays MP3, FLAC, ALAC, WAV, OGG and AAC, and adds lyrics, playlists, themes and advanced sound controls.
Google Drive access is optional and is used only when you connect a personal Drive folder. UNIFY browses the folder you choose, reads supported audio, metadata and artwork, and streams or caches music for playback. It cannot create, upload, edit, rename, move, share or delete your Drive files.
Yes. Connect a personal cloud folder, compatible WebDAV server or SMB2/SMB3 share. UNIFY scans supported audio, reads metadata and artwork, and adds the selected source to one library.
Remote songs start after an adaptive buffer and continue downloading sequentially while playback reads completed bytes. You can choose cache limits, network preferences, keep completed songs after disconnect and clear cache at any time.
Themes include Cyber Cyan, Synthwave, Aurora, Obsidian Gold, Ember Beat, Firework Night, Galaxy Portal, Snow Crystal, Rose Bloom, Neon Rain, Sunrise Morning, Tet Firework Rays and Celestial Opal.
UNIFY includes 3D 360°, 10D 360°, 7.1 Surround, Dome 360°, 31D 360°, Pulse and motion paths such as Orbit, Spiral, Pendulum, Chaos, Magic 8, Heartbeat, Zigzag and Bounce.
Google Drive access is read-only. Removing a saved source library or clearing audio cache affects local app data only and does not delete the original music files from Google Drive, Dropbox, WebDAV or SMB/NAS.
