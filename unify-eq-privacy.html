Privacy Policy

This page applies to UNIFY Equalizer Bass Booster (also referred to as UNIFY EQ). This policy explains what UNIFY EQ accesses, why it is needed, where information is processed and the controls available to you.

Last updated · July 16, 2026 · Version 3.0

01Introduction

UNIFY EQ is an Android equalizer, sound-effects and local music playback application operated by an independent developer in Vietnam. This policy applies to the Android app and this website.

UNIFY EQ does not provide a public streaming catalog. Its core sound processing is designed to run on the user’s Android device.

Core principle: original local audio files are not uploaded to a developer-operated service for equalization, effects or playback.

02Local media and Android access

Data or permissionPurpose
Audio and media filesScan, organize and play supported music stored on your phone or supported SD card. The app may read filenames, paths, tags, duration and embedded artwork.
Audio processing or audio session accessApply equalizer, dynamics, visualizer, spatial and sound-effect functions on the device. This permission is not used to create voice recordings.
Notifications and foreground playbackShow playback controls and keep supported audio functions active in the background.
Local app storageStore settings, playlists, profiles, presets, artwork, metadata, editor segments and local backups.

03On-device sound processing

Equalizer values, gain settings, dynamics parameters, limiter settings, spatial-effect controls, reverb choices and visualizer data are processed locally to provide the sound selected by the user.

UNIFY EQ may save presets, listening profiles, per-song or per-segment configurations and interface preferences on the device. You can remove this local information by clearing app data or uninstalling the app.

Audio content: the app does not sell, license or share your original music files with advertisers or data brokers.

04Optional accounts and Community

The equalizer and local player can be used without publishing your music. If you sign in to the optional Community, UNIFY EQ may process information needed to provide social preset features:

  • Name, email address, profile image, username, authentication provider and unique account identifier.
  • Optional country, biography, creator tier, follower/following relationships and online or recently active status.
  • Equalizer presets or Music Editor Pro segment configurations that you intentionally upload, including names, descriptions, device and genre tags, generated or selected cover images and moderation status.
  • Downloads, likes, favorites, comments, follows, reposts, reports, notifications and other community interactions.
  • Premium entitlement status, without the developer receiving your full payment-card details.

Community account and published-content data may be stored using Google Firebase services. Uploaded presets and profile information can be visible to other users. Original music files are not included in a community preset upload unless a future feature clearly asks for a separate audio upload and obtains your permission.

Local-first distinction: your songs stay on your Android device; only the preset, profile or interaction data you choose to publish is sent to the online Community.

05Diagnostics, analytics, advertising and billing

  • Diagnostics: app version, device/app state and crash information may be processed to maintain stability and security.
  • Analytics: aggregate or pseudonymous events may be used to understand feature performance and improve the app.
  • Advertising: the free version may display ads where enabled. Advertising providers may process device or advertising identifiers under their own policies and applicable consent settings.
  • Billing: purchases and subscriptions are processed by Google Play. UNIFY EQ receives purchase or entitlement status, not full payment-card details.

UNIFY EQ does not sell your email address, personal presets or local music files.

06Service providers, public content and disclosure

Information may be processed by providers that support app operation, including Google Firebase for optional accounts and Community features, Google Play for billing and distribution, and crash, analytics or advertising services where enabled.

Presets, segment configurations, profile details, comments and other content that you intentionally publish are designed to be visible to Community users and may be shared through generated cards or links. Reports may be reviewed for moderation, safety and policy enforcement.

We may disclose information when required by law, to protect users or the service, or during a legitimate business transfer. Original local music files are not shared with advertisers or data brokers.

07Security, storage and retention

  • Local music indexes, downloaded presets, profiles, playlists and editor projects remain on the device until cleared or removed.
  • Community account information and published content are retained while the account or content is active and for a reasonable period needed for security, moderation, legal compliance or deletion processing.
  • Deleted community content may remain briefly in backups, abuse-prevention logs or moderation records before routine removal.
  • Diagnostic records and anonymized or aggregated statistics may be retained for a limited operational period.
  • Reasonable technical and organizational safeguards are used, but no security method is perfect.

08Your choices and controls

  • Use local equalizer and playback features without creating a community account.
  • Change Android permissions through system settings.
  • Clear presets, profiles, playlists, editor segments or other local data in the app where available.
  • Clear all local app data through Android settings or uninstall the app.
  • Request access, correction or deletion of optional account information.
  • Manage subscriptions through Google Play.

09Children

UNIFY EQ is not directed to children under the minimum age required by applicable law for online account services. A parent or guardian who believes a child supplied personal information may contact us to request review and deletion.
